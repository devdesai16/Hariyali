Ask The Expert

Why are my flowers falling or dying?

Improper watering is often the reason for sudden dying of plants.
If you forgot to water for a few days, it's possible that the roots dried up.
However, the opposite is more likely, as too much water is often to blame for dying container plants.

What causes leaves to become pale?

It may be caused by reduced blood flow and oxygen or by a decreased number of red blood cells.
It can be a manifestation of emotions such as fear (“pale as a ghost”), or it can be a symptom of serious medical conditions such as severe anemia, bloodstream infection, or frostbite.

What causes brown crispy leaves?

Browning leaves are typically caused by under watering, sunburn, or overwatering.
If the leaf tips are turning brown and crunchy, the soil likely became too dry for too long in between waterings. This can also cause the plant to drop leaves.

How do I change the pots?

1. Pour a layer of fresh, pre-moistened mix in the planter you're potting into, and pack it down.
2. Remove the plant from the current pot.
3. Loosen and prune the roots.
4. Gently unbind any loose roots.
5. Set plant in new planter.
6. Add mix.
7. Even it out.
8. You're all set!

Why are gnats flying around my plant?

The most common cause of fungus gnats in plants is overwatering, so be sure to let your soil dry out in between waterings as often as possible.
Check the soil with your finger every few days, and once you feel it starting to get dry, leave it like that for about a week to keep gnats away.
